What we wish we knew before our first year at FHS

I wish I was not as nervous about going into High-school!!! I know thats a very stereotypical thing to say, but, I promise the high-school isn't as intimidating as it seems!

-Issy Baumann

Don’t stress your self out about grades and test scores so much. They matter of course, but putting unneeded pressure on yourself will only make it worse. As long as you try your best, there is literally nothing else you can do. Another thing, communicate with teachers and try to build strong relationships with them. Even with some of my most difficult teachers, sending them an email has never failed.

-Anonymous

If you are considering the STEM/Art endorsement, start thinking about it soon. I was planning on getting the STEM one, but by the time I thought about it I was too far behind.

-Ash Lane

I wish I had known a little more about the work load for the classes, freshmen year the work isn’t too bad even if you are in mainly honors classes so I wish I had signed up for more courses.

-Anonymous

Be prepared mentally, make sure you pick your friends right, don’t worry too much, don’t be to hard on yourself, always reach out if In need.

-Seth Warner

Don't stress too much about Earth & Space Science: one grade isn't going to ruin your life. Be confident during class and ask questions(no bad questions! Someone is probably wondering the same thing, so take the risk and be proactive). Signing up for study halls isn't a cardinal sin - they can be good opportunities to get ahead of work for busy students and athletes, or just time to take a break during school. Upperclassmen are lit. Don't feel guilty for not always doing homework or becoming a research scientist engineer pianist debate champion ballet superstar 24/7(it's ok to have fun in highschool).

-Kevin Lu

Honors classes aren’t as challenging as you think! I feel like middle school teachers made honors classes seem like these very scary and much higher level classes and that discouraged a lot of people from taking them. The intensity of honors classes all depend on the teacher, the subject, and your overall interest in the subject. In my experience the more I like a subject the more work I am willing to put into it. So if there is a subject you really don’t like and have trouble focusing during you don’t have to take an honors level! For example, I really don’t like history it is very boring to me and I usually avoid studying for the tests until the last minute because the subjects never interest me. So I didn’t take honors history and I instead took honors level courses and eventually AP for courses that did actually interest me. That being said though honors are not everything. It is better to succeed in an CP class than to struggle in an honors class. I know it is early but I also know some are already thinking about what to do to look good for colleges and my best advice is to just focus on yourself. Don’t take honors or AP classes that you know you will heavily struggle with just to be able to say you took them. Because surprise surprise colleges would rather see As and Bs in CP classes than Cs and below in honor classes!! And if you are unsure if you can handle the honors course start with CP, if you find it is too easy you can always move up!

-Anonymous

I wish I knew that trying to conform to Falmouth’s environment and social standards isn’t always the way to go. Learning to express yourself and not caring what people think is much more important

-Anonymous

It’s easy to get caught up in caring too much about being the best in class or comparing your grades with other peoples, but try to just focus on yourself, if you are understanding the class or just trying your hardest. Who the smartest person in your class was isn’t going to matter in the future, but how much work you were putting into the class will.

-Anonymous

Advice for in person/virtual/hybrid learning

Be yourself. At the end of the day, no one cares as much as you think about little things you might worry about or be self-conscious about. Surround yourself with people who make you feel good, and not people who make you feel “cool” or who “fit in”. As cheesy as all of this sounds, I seriously can’t stress this enough, and it’s something I wish someone had told me as a freshman.

I remember walking into school, freshman year, sometime before midterms and I don’t remember much but I do remember just resting my head on the table and crying in the middle of the downstairs commons amongst my friends. After that day I didn’t attend school for the rest of the year because I was hospitalized for suicidal possibility. After that I didn’t attend school until the following year. at that point I had realized I overdid myself stressing about how much work I was doing, If I was doing enough, to much. Stressing about my grades, my social life. What I’m trying to get at here is that sometimes you just need to not overthink things, always reach out to a teacher, a friend, family, if needed. That’s what I was lacking, I was always a “I can do it myself” kind of guy, but look where that lead me. If you struggle with mental health issues like I do, then please reach out, it is a real struggle if you go alone.
